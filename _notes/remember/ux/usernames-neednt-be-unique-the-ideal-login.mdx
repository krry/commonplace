# Usernames Needn't Be Unique - The Ideal Login

I gather from all the chest-thumping about usernames I've overheard that many users still expect to be utterly unique in an online population quickly approaching that of the globe. It's understandable that such newly archaic expectations would persist well beyond their utility to their environment, but that doesn't make it excusable or advisable.

(As the rate of technological development continues to accelerate beyond the typical human's pace of learning, the proportion of tired paradigms that should be retired paradigms will only increase.)

Introducing user-facing multi-variant namespaces...

Users are given a unique id number. The UID should be long enough that you will not foreseeably deplete the list in the course of your operation, and ordered meaningfully, whether by seniority, geography, randomly for security, or some mixture of these.
