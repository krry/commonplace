# Theravada

## [[brahmavihārās]] - The Divine Abodes

## Pāramī(tā)s

Pāramīs are excellences, while pāramitās are perfections. Pāra "the far bank" with mita "that which has arrive" could assemble to mean "arrivals beyond" or "transcendences", as the Tibetan Buddhists' take it.

### 10 pāramitās of Theravada (Pali)

1. dāna - generosity
2. sīla - ethics, morality, Right Conduct
3. nekkhamma - detachment, Right Intention
4. paññā - wisdom, realizing anicca, dukkha, anatta; same as prajñā
5. viriya - energy, enthusiasm, effort, diligence; same as vīrya
6. khanti - patience, forbearance, forgiveness
7. sacca - real, true
8. adhiṭṭhāna - resolve, willpower
9. mettā - loving-kindness, benevolence
10. upekkhā - equanimity, impartiality

### 6 pāramitās of Mahayana Buddhism (Sanskrit)

1. dāna - generosity
2. sīla - right performance and right avoidance
3. kṣānti - patient forgiveness
4. vīrya - virtuous enthusiasm
5. dhyāna - perfect awareness; meditation; same as jhāna
6. prajñā - wise insight; seeing the true nature of reality
